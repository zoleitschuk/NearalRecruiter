Associate Clinical Development Engineer - 160908 

Description

 

“Building the bridge between clinical and technical to shape future surgical technologies”

 

Primary Function of Position:

The Associate Clinical Development Engineer (CDE) gains an in-depth knowledge of the da Vinci® Surgical System to work with design engineers and clinicians to develop safe and highly effective products for our multi-port surgical robotic system platforms.  The CDE is responsible for establishing and validating data-driven clinical requirements to ensure a high quality patient outcome and for conveying the clinical value throughout the organization.

 

Roles and Responsibilities:

New Product Development:
  • Support clinical development engineering in assessments of surgical system prototypes:
    • Conduct research though literature searches, reviewing prior test reports, watching surgeries and collecting data from videos
    • Develop models to simulate the necessary environment for evaluations
    • Characterize the surgical performance of prototypes
    • Analyze and synthesize results to product development team
  • Collaborating closely with product development teams to define clinical performance goals and potential risks for new & existing products
  • Learn about design control and how to assess and document clinical risks
  • Collaborate with other organizations within the company as needed: regulatory, human factors, training, quality, manufacturing, marketing and sales
 
Clinical Laboratory & Field Work
  • Learn the vast clinical landscape that our products serve
  • Conduct design iteration assessments of products both internally and with external key opinion leaders
  • Support formal validation testing for new products, product updates and modifications to existing designs, to ensure the clinical safety and efficacy of the product
  • Support clinical evaluation of new or enhanced products at key customer sites
  • Identify potential product enhancements & new product ideas by applying an analytical approach to understanding the clinical needs of surgeons
Other
  • Keeps the organization’s vision and values at the forefront of decision making and action
  • Communicates findings in a timely and organized manner to cross-functional teams within the organization

 

Qualifications

 

Skill/Job Requirements:

 

To adequately perform the responsibilities of this position the individual must have:

  • Minimum of B.S. in Mechanical Engineering, Biomedical Engineering or similar
  • Experience in medical device industry and/or working with surgeons in an operating room environment desired
  • Exposure to surgical terminology, anatomy, and surgical technique
  • Excellent mechanical aptitude, and intuition for spatial relations, including the ability to understand complex mechanical products
  • Excellent analytical capability and practical skills to develop and test a thesis and draw succinct conclusions (i.e. perform literature/patent searches; test fixture and model design and development using Labview, Matlab, Solidworks; analyze data using Matlab, Minitab, Excel, SAS) 
  • Excellent teamwork skills with proven ability to cultivate collaboration across multiple disciplines to insure timely project success
  • Effective communication skills (verbal, written, presentation)
  • Proven success recognizing critical issues and driving them to closure by taking coordinated action
  • Self-starter needing minimal supervision
  • Self-confident and able to react quickly under pressure
  • Ability to travel up to 25% of the time, internationally and domestic
